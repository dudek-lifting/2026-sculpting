Scenario

A Yahoo Mail customer is locked out of their email account and calls the contact center for help. The agent verifies the caller using non-sensitive methods, diagnoses the lockout cause, initiates account recovery steps, and confirms restored access or next steps.

Assumptions

  • The account lockout was triggered by unusual sign-in activity or multiple failed password attempts.
  • The agent has access to internal account recovery tools and status indicators.
  • Verification is performed without collecting sensitive personal data.
  • Brand colors are inferred from public branding; exact hex overrides can be provided if desired.

Agent Prep

  • Be ready to explain the recovery process clearly and calmly.
  • Confirm the caller understands any security-related limitations.
  • Have placeholders ready for recovery email or phone confirmation.

Interaction Script

Agent: Thank you for calling Yahoo Support. My name is {{agent_name}}. Before we get started, {{call_recording_disclosure}}. How can I help you today?

Caller: I’m locked out of my Yahoo email and can’t sign in.

Agent: I’m sorry you’re dealing with that. I know email access is important. I’ll help you get back in as quickly as possible. To start, can you confirm the Yahoo email address you’re trying to access?

Caller: It’s {{email_address}}.

System: Agent locates account and views lockout status {{account_status}}.

Agent: Thank you. I see the account is temporarily locked for security reasons. I’ll ask a couple of verification questions so we can begin recovery.

Agent: Please confirm the recovery email or phone number ending in {{recovery_hint}}.

Caller: Yes, that looks right.

System: Agent initiates account recovery workflow {{recovery_flow_id}}.

Agent: I’ve sent a one-time verification step to your recovery option. Let me know once you’ve completed it.

Caller: Okay, I’ve done that.

System: Account access restored {{resolution_code}}.

Agent: Great news—you should now be able to sign in. Please try logging in while we’re on the call.

Caller: I’m back in. Thank you!

Agent: You’re welcome! To help prevent this in the future, I recommend reviewing your security settings once we finish. Is there anything else I can help you with today?

Caller: No, that’s all.

Agent: Thanks for calling Yahoo Support. Have a great day!

Branches & Variations

Recovery option not accessible Common Exception

Agent: If you no longer have access to that recovery option, we can try an alternate method or submit a manual review. I’ll explain what happens next and the expected timeframe.

System: Manual review initiated {{manual_review_id}}.

Caller frustrated or upset Objection

Agent: I understand how frustrating this is, and I appreciate your patience. I’m going to stay with you and do everything I can to move this forward.

Data Capture

Field Example / Placeholder Purpose
Email Address {{email_address}} Identify the affected Yahoo Mail account
Account Status {{account_status}} Confirm lockout reason and eligibility
Recovery Flow ID {{recovery_flow_id}} Track recovery actions taken
Resolution Code {{resolution_code}} Document outcome of the interaction

Compliance & Disclosures

Use approved verification methods only. Do not request passwords, full phone numbers, or other sensitive personal data. Provide {{call_recording_disclosure}} at the start of the interaction.

After-Interaction

  • Confirm notes are saved under case {{case_id}}.
  • Ensure recovery or escalation status is documented.
  • Send follow-up confirmation if applicable.
